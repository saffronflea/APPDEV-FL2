TYPES OF RICE

There are many types of rice in the world, each of which has its own taste, texture and cooking method. For example, white rice is a popular choice in most households because it is bland and easy to cook. In contrast, brown rice is healthy and healthy because of its high fiber content. Known for its delicate flavor and slightly sticky texture, jasmine rice is perfect for Asian cuisine, adding a light flavor that complements a variety of ingredients. Long grain, aromatic basmati rice is ideal for pilaf, giving it a unique flavor and texture. As the name suggests, glutinous rice is super very sticky and is often used in savory sweet desserts, especially in Southeast Asian cuisine. Italian rice varieties such as Arborio rice have a sweet texture that is perfect for risotto. Due to the sweet and salty nature of the grain, it is often mixed with other types of rice to add flavor and complexity to the food. Each type of rice brings its own characteristics to the table, enhancing the unique products of different cultures. Whether it's a simple steamed rice dish or a complex dish, the choice of rice can have a big impact on the overall taste and texture.



SINANGDOMENG

    Sinangdomeng rice is known for its delicate flavor and low fat content. This is due to its reliable quality in most Filipino households and its adaptability to a variety of dishes, from simple steamed rice to more complex recipes.

DINORADO

    Dinarado rice has a very pleasant aroma and flavor. It has a soft and slightly gooey texture that makes it popular for special occasions and holiday meals. This premium variety is often compared to jasmine rice for its quality.

WAG-WAG

    Wag-wag rice is preferred if it is firmly cooked and less oily than other varieties. Used in everyday dishes, it is appreciated for its inexpensive and consistent cooking qualities.

MILAGROSA

    Milagrosa rice is firm and flavorful with a slightly sticky and mild taste. This variety is often prepared for festivals and celebrations because of its delicious taste and flavour, similar to jasmine rice, but with a distinctly local character.

MALAGKIT

    Malagkit rice is very sticky when cooked. Mostly used in Filipino desserts like bingka, suman and biko. The different fat content makes these traditional sweet potatoes difficult to stir fry.

BLACK RICE

    Also known as “Forbidden rice”, black rice is packed with antioxidants and nutrients. It is a dark brown color that turns purple when cooked and has a slightly sweet and nutty taste. It is commonly used in foods and desserts due to its wonderful appearance and health benefits.

JASMINE

    Jasmine rice is a long grain rice known for its aromatic flavor and slightly chewy texture. Its smooth, creamy consistency and subtle sweet flavor make it perfect for Asian dishes such as curries and stir-fries. It is widely used in the Philippines and adds flavor to different dishes.
