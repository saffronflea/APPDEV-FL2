CULTIVATIONS

Rice cultivation is hard process, it takes a lot of work. Crop success depends on climate, soil, water availability, pest management and cultivation. The growth of rice depends on the rainfall and warm weather conditions in which rice grows. However, poor climate and weather can damage, lose or damage crops. Good soil is essential for agriculture. Good soil can help crops grow, while poor soil slows them down. Access to water is very important. Successful growth of rice fields requires soil water management. Use of chemicals is necessary to control crops from pests and diseases. In addition to these environmental factors, external factors such as climate change, government policies and economic stability of farmers affect rice production. Climate change affects weather and rice production. Government policies provide incentives for rice production through financing, regulations, or support programs. WEATHER

    Definition: Weather and temperature play an important role in agriculture, as does rainfall. The best weather is warm, with plenty of rain, but the worst is drought or floods that destroy crops.

SOIL QUALITY

    Fertile soil is essential for growing healthy crops. Soil quality can greatly affect the ability of nutrients to reach the roots, which can greatly affect the performance and health of the rice.

WATER AVAILABILITY

    Water production is important for crops. Successful rice cultivation requires large flooded amounts of water, and inadequate irrigation can produce little or no crops.

PESTS

    Improper management of insects, diseases and weeds can damage crops. Seed protection and seed quality practices are recommended.

FARMING PRACTICES

    Agricultural activities including ploughing, cultivation, harvesting, etc. rice production will be greatly affected. Good cultivation means high harvest and yield of rice

CLIMATE CHANGE

    Effects of Climate Change Unforeseen movements, such as changes in rainfall and temperature, can affect pig growth and productivity.

FARMERS' FINANCES

   The wealth of farmers depends on how much money they invest in quality seeds, new fertilizers and new farm equipment. Capital is critical to a successful agriculture.
